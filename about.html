About

Creator

Uzzie T. Cannon is an educator and photography enthusiast. She developed the HAAPO after realizing that she knew of only one African American photographer, Gordon Parks. Long a fan of his work, Cannon began researching leisurely other AA photographers and discovered a significant number who have not been highlighted in the history of the art. When knowledge in website design and new skills in digital humanities, Cannon decided to create History of African American Photographers Online to expose others to the lives and art of African American Photographers.

Content

HAAPO catalogs general information on African American photographers. Such information includes basic biography, style, process, subjects, and his/her most productive period. It is not intended to be exhaustive by including every photographer that has ever lived. However, the more I discover, the more I will include as long as the artist has made a significant contribution to the art form. I have also included resources to help interested individuals learn the basics of photography to perhaps develop an interest in the medium

Sources

Much of the content provided on HAAPO is derived from the work of noted scholar and photographer, Deborah Willis. Her seminal texts Black Photographers 1840-1940: A Bio-Bibliography; Reflections in Black: A History of Black Photographers 1840 to the Present; and Posing Beauty: African American Images from the 1890s to the Present chronicle the origins and dveelopement of photography among African Americans. I also studied Ross J. Kelbaugh's Introduction to African American Photographs, which proved invaluable in recognizing the various processes of image development throughout history. These sources' bibliographic information can be found on the Resources page of the site.
